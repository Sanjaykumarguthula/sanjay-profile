Create Stunning Color Schemes

Our Color Palette Generator helps you find harmonious color combinations for your website, app, presentation, or any design project. Select a base color, and the tool will generate palettes based on color theory principles.

How It Works

  1. Pick Your Base Color: Use the color picker or enter a HEX code for your desired starting color.
  2. Generate Palettes: Click the "Generate Palettes" button.
  3. Explore Palette Types: The tool will display several types of palettes:
    • Monochromatic: Variations in lightness and saturation of a single color.
    • Analogous: Colors that are next to each other on the color wheel.
    • Complementary: Colors opposite each other on the color wheel, creating high contrast.
    • Split-Complementary: A variation of complementary, using two colors adjacent to its complement.
    • Triadic: Three colors evenly spaced around the color wheel.
    • Tetradic (Rectangle): Four colors arranged into two complementary pairs.
  4. Copy & Use Color Codes: Click on any color swatch in the generated palettes to copy its HEX code to your clipboard.

Understanding Color Theory

Color theory is a set of principles used to create harmonious color combinations. Understanding these concepts can significantly improve your design work. Our tool leverages these principles to provide you with well-balanced and visually appealing color schemes.

Frequently Asked Questions (Color Palette Generator)

Complementary colors are pairs of colors which, when combined or mixed, cancel each other out (lose hue) by producing a grayscale color like white or black. When placed next to each other, they create the strongest contrast for those two colors. For example, red and green are complementary colors.

Currently, this tool allows you to copy the HEX codes of individual colors by clicking on them. You can then paste these codes into your design software or notes. We are considering options for exporting full palettes in the future.

Choosing a good base color depends on your project's branding, target audience, and the mood you want to convey. Consider the emotions associated with different colors (e.g., blue for trust, green for nature/growth). You can also draw inspiration from existing designs you like or use a color that represents your brand identity.
