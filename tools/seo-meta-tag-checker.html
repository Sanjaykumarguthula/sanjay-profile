Analyze Your Meta Tags

Meta tags provide search engines with important information about your web pages. Optimizing them is a crucial step in on-page SEO. Our tool helps you identify key meta tags present in your HTML and highlights potential areas for improvement.

What Meta Tags Does This Tool Check?

  • Title Tag: The main title of your page displayed in search results and browser tabs. Checks for presence and length.
  • Meta Description: A brief summary of your page's content, often shown in search results. Checks for presence and length.
  • Meta Keywords: Though less impactful for major search engines now, it checks for their presence.
  • Viewport Tag: Essential for mobile responsiveness, ensuring your site adapts to different screen sizes.
  • Open Graph (OG) Tags: Crucial for how your content appears when shared on social media platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, etc. (e.g., `og:title`, `og:description`, `og:image`, `og:url`, `og:type`).

How to Use the Meta Tag Checker

  1. Open the webpage you want to analyze in your browser.
  2. Right-click on the page and select "View Page Source" (or similar option depending on your browser).
  3. Copy the entire HTML source code from the new tab/window that opens.
  4. Paste the copied HTML code into the input area above.
  5. Click the "Analyze Meta Tags" button.
  6. Review the analysis results, which will show found tags, their content, and basic recommendations.

Note: This tool analyzes the HTML you provide. It does not crawl live URLs directly due to client-side browser restrictions (CORS).

Why Are Meta Tags Important for SEO?

Meta tags help search engines understand the content and context of your pages, which can influence rankings. Well-crafted title tags and meta descriptions can also improve click-through rates (CTR) from search results by providing compelling snippets to users.
