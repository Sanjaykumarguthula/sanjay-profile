How to Play Minesweeper

  1. Select your desired difficulty level (Beginner, Intermediate, or Expert) or start with the default.
  2. Click the "Reset" button (Smiley face) or a difficulty button to start a new game.
  3. Left-click on a square to reveal it:
    • If you reveal a mine , the game is over!
    • If the square is empty, it will show a number. This number indicates how many mines are hidden in the 8 squares immediately surrounding it.
    • If an empty square has no adjacent mines (a '0'), all adjacent empty squares will automatically be revealed.
  4. Right-click (or long-press on mobile/touch devices) on a square to place or remove a flag . Flags are used to mark squares you suspect contain mines.
  5. The "Mines" counter shows how many mines are left to be flagged (it decreases as you place flags).
  6. The objective is to reveal all squares that do not contain mines. You win when all non-mine squares are opened.

Frequently Asked Questions (Minesweeper)

A number on a revealed square tells you exactly how many mines are hidden in the eight squares immediately surrounding that number (horizontally, vertically, and diagonally). If a square shows a '0' (or is blank), it means there are no mines in any of its adjacent squares.

To flag a square you suspect contains a mine, right-click on it with your mouse. On touch devices, you can usually long-press the square to place a flag. Right-clicking or long-pressing again will remove the flag.

In many versions of Minesweeper, the first click is guaranteed to be safe (not a mine), and often it's an empty square that reveals a larger area. This implementation will aim for a safe first click.
